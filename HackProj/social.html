    • Social Aspects:

    Modernization efforts refer to deliberate and systematic initiatives aimed at bringing about social, economic, technological, and cultural progress and development within a society or nation. These efforts typically involve implementing changes and reforms to align with contemporary standards and practices.

    Social learning is a theory that suggests people learn by observing, imitating, and interacting with others. It emphasizes the importance of social interactions, relationships, and cultural influences in the learning process. This concept has been widely studied in psychology and education to understand how individuals acquire new skills, knowledge, and behaviors through social interactions with peers, teachers, and the environment. Social learning can occur in various settings, including classrooms, workplaces, and everyday life.

    Formal education refers to structured, organized, and systematic learning that takes place within institutions like schools, colleges, and universities. It typically follows a curriculum and is guided by educators or teachers. Formal education provides a standardized and recognized path for individuals to acquire knowledge, skills, and qualifications. It often includes grades and certifications, which can be essential for career advancement and personal development. Formal education systems vary from one country to another but are a fundamental part of society's efforts to prepare individuals for various roles in the workforce and society.

    Political socialization is the process by which individuals acquire their political beliefs, values, attitudes, and knowledge about the political system. This lifelong process begins in childhood and continues throughout a person's life. Several agents contribute to political socialization, including family, peers, schools, media, and religious institutions.
