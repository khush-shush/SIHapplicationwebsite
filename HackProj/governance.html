    • Governance:-

    Risk Management: Effective governance should include risk management practices that identify, assess, and mitigate ESG-related risks that could impact the company's financial performance or reputation.Stakeholder Engagement: Companies are expected to engage with various stakeholders, including employees, customers, communities, and investors, to understand their concerns and incorporate their input into decision-making processes.Board Oversight: The board of directors is responsible for overseeing the company's ESG efforts and ensuring that ESG considerations are integrated into the overall corporate strategy.Compliance with Regulations: Governance within ESG requires companies to comply with relevant laws and regulations related to environmental protection, labor rights, and ethical business practices.Long-Term Focus: ESG governance emphasizes a long-term perspective, encouraging companies to consider the impact of their actions on society and the environment over time, rather than focusing solely on short-term financial gains.Effective ESG governance demonstrates a company's commitment to responsible and sustainable business practices, which can enhance its reputation, attract socially responsible investors, and mitigate potential ESG-related risks.
